How to use

  • Text ➔ Morse: Type in the top textarea. The Morse translation appears instantly in both output boxes.
  • Morse ➔ Text: Paste Morse into the lower textarea. Use one space between letters and a forward slash / between words.
  • Play Sound: Sends the Morse to your speakers. The first time you use the app, click anywhere on the page to unlock the browser's audio.
  • Flashlight: Blinks your phone's rear LED in Morse. Requires HTTPS, camera permission and a device with a torch (Android Chrome; iOS Safari does not expose the torch).
  • Export WAV: Renders the Morse as an audio file and downloads it.
  • Settings: Speed in Words Per Minute, Frequency in Hz (tone pitch) and Volume in dB.
  • Clear All: Empties every textarea in one click.

Install as an app on your phone

Recommended: install this page as a Progressive Web App so it launches full-screen from your home screen, works offline-friendly and hides the browser chrome.

Android (Chrome, Edge, Brave)

  1. Open the app URL in Chrome.
  2. Tap the ⋮ menu (top-right).
  3. Choose Install app (or Add to Home screen).
  4. Confirm Install. An icon appears on your home screen.

iPhone / iPad (Safari)

  1. Open the app URL in Safari (not Chrome — iOS requires Safari for install).
  2. Tap the Share icon (the square with an upward arrow).
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Give it a name and tap Add.

Launch it from the home-screen icon and it runs like a native app.

Supported languages & characters

Every basic Latin letter, digit and standard punctuation mark, plus accented characters from the following languages:

  • Nordic: Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, Finnish, Icelandic, Faroese — Å Ä Ö Ø Æ Ð Þ Ý É Ú Ó Í Á
  • Germanic: German, Dutch — Ä Ö Ü ß Ë
  • French & Romance: French, Spanish, Portuguese, Italian, Catalan, Romanian — À Á Â Ã Ç É È Ê Ë Î Ï Ñ Ó Ô Œ Ù Ú Û Ò Ă Ș Ț
  • Central European / Slavic: Polish, Czech, Slovak, Slovene, Croatian, Serbian — Ą Ć Ę Ł Ń Ś Ź Ż Č Š Ž Ě Ň Đ
  • Hungarian: Á É Í Ó Ö Ő Ú Ü Ű
  • Turkish / Azerbaijani: Ç Ğ İ Ö Ş Ü
  • Baltic: Estonian, Latvian, Lithuanian — Ä Ö Ü Č Š Ž
  • Esperanto: Ĉ Ĝ Ĥ Ĵ Ŝ Ŭ

Codes follow ITU-R M.1677-1 and standard national extensions. Where several letters share the same code (e.g. Ä / Æ / Ą all encode as .-.-), the app decodes to the most widely-used European variant. Lowercase input is accepted; ß maps to its own code ...--.. instead of being expanded to "SS".

Tips

  • Unknown characters are rendered as ? in the output.
  • Standard Morse timing follows the PARIS convention: dash = 3 × dot, letter gap = 3 × dot, word gap = 7 × dot.
  • For long messages, lower the speed to 15 WPM to make it easier to follow by ear.
